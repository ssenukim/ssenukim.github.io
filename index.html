About

Seonwoo Kim is a graduate researcher specializing in robotic manipulation at Korea University, where he completed his Master's degree under the supervision of Prof. Daehie Hong. His research focuses on classical control theory and learning-based control for robotic manipulators, with particular emphasis on disturbance observer design and force estimation algorithms. He has published first-author papers in IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters (RA-L) and IEEE Transactions on Instrumentation and Measurement (TIM). Recently, he has expanded his focus to AI-driven robot learning at the Korea Institute of Science and Technology (KIST), exploring how imitation and reinforcement learning can enable complex manipulation behaviors in humanoid robots.

Research Interests

His research focuses on robotic manipulation, with particular emphasis on classical control theory and learning-based control for robotic manipulators. During his Master's studies, he developed a strong foundation in disturbance observer design and force estimation algorithms. More recently, he has expanded his focus to AI-driven robot learning, exploring how imitation and reinforcement learning can enable complex manipulation behaviors.

He is particularly interested in contact-rich manipulation and whole-body manipulation, where he aims to bridge model-based control with learning-based approaches to achieve robust and dexterous robot manipulation in unstructured environments.
